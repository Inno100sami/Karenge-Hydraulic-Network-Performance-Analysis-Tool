⚠️ Pressure Management Recommendations
  • PRV Installation (NYARWIRE2, NZIAKARE2, NYABIHRE1/2): Zones with pressure >21 bar should be fitted with Pressure Reducing Valves (PRVs) to bring operating pressure within the 10–15 bar WHO/WASAC target range.
  • Booster Pump for Very Low Zones: KARBICRE6, NYAGATRE4, NYAGATRE5 and NYARWIRE1 (0–5 bar) need either additional pump lift or a dedicated booster to meet minimum service pressure of 0.5 bar at consumer taps.
  • Gahuka Zone (GAHKIBRE1 – 28.4 bar · Critical): Highest-pressure zone in the network. Immediate PRV installation and pipe-class upgrade (PN 16 minimum) recommended to prevent pipe ruptures.
  • Leakage Mapping: High-pressure zones (16–25+ bar) account for an estimated 30–50% of Non-Revenue Water — field mapping of leakage points in these zones should be the next priority.

💡 Pipe Network Recommendations
  • Ø25 mm Dominance (67%): Over two-thirds of all pipes are small-bore 25 mm service connections. These should be surveyed for age and condition — PVC pipes older than 20–25 years are prone to UV degradation and brittle fracture.
  • Velocity Check on Ø25–32 mm pipes: At average flows, velocities in small-bore pipes may fall below 0.3 m/s — below the minimum for self-cleansing, risking sediment accumulation and water quality deterioration.
  • Upsizing candidate corridors: Where Ø25–32 mm pipes feed clusters of more than 50 demand nodes, upsizing to Ø50 mm should be evaluated to reduce headloss and improve residual pressure.

✅ Pump Station Performance Recommendations
  • Energy efficiency audit: With 11 pumps running against heads of 200–240 m, pump efficiency should be verified against design curves. A 5% efficiency gain across all stations would save ~15–20 kWh/day.
  • Parallel pump scheduling: The Karenge-Nyabihu station (7 pumps) should be operated in on/off sequences tied to reservoir levels to avoid simultaneous running at low-efficiency part-load conditions.
  • Gahuka booster (6 L/s): The single-pump station has no redundancy. A standby unit should be installed to prevent service interruption to the GAHKIB zone on pump failure.

🔴 Storage & Supply Continuity Recommendations
  • Storage adequacy review: Many tanks have diameters of 10–15 m with max levels of 1.5–2.5 m, yielding usable volumes of 120–440 m³. For zones serving 500–1,500 people this provides only 6–12 hours of storage — below the 24-hour target.
  • Critical zones — KARRUGRE1 (Ø200 m): The main reservoir at 1,404 m feeds the entire network downstream. Its condition and level monitoring should be prioritised; a low-level alarm and automatic pump cut-off is strongly recommended.
  • GAHKIB zone tanks (elev >1,630 m): Three tanks at extreme elevation may run dry during peak demand if pump delivery falls short. Real-time SCADA level monitoring is recommended.
  • Tank mixing model: All 32 tanks use the MIXED model (complete mixing). A water-age analysis should be run to identify dead-end zones where water may exceed 48-hour residence time.
